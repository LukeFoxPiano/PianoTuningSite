About Luke Fox

A Technician Who Listens

Piano tuning is part science, part ear training, and part relationship with an instrument. I take all three seriously.

Background

Who I Am

I'm Luke Fox, a piano technician based in Dacono, Colorado, serving homes, studios, churches, and schools throughout Northern Colorado. I've been working on pianos for years — drawn to the craft by a fascination with acoustic instruments and a belief that a well-maintained piano is a joy to play and a poorly maintained one is a genuine obstacle.

I came to piano technology through music. Playing the piano first gave me an intimate understanding of what pianists actually need from their instrument: consistent touch, clear tone, and reliable response across the full range of the keyboard. That perspective informs everything I do on a service call.

I work on all types of acoustic pianos — uprights, studio verticals, baby grands, and concert grands — from everyday home instruments to performance pianos that need to be ready for an audience.

Registered Piano Technician (RPT) — member of the Piano Technicians Guild. RPT certification requires passing three rigorous exams covering tuning accuracy, technical knowledge, and regulation skills.

My Approach

How I Work

Every service call starts with a conversation. Before I open the lid, I want to know how the piano is used, what concerns the owner has, and what the instrument means to the household. That context shapes every decision I make.

I use both electronic tuning devices and aural tuning techniques, cross-checking one against the other. Electronic tools give precision; trained ears catch things that meters miss — the subtle beating between strings, the way a note sustains, the way harmonics stack in a chord. Together they produce a result that is both accurate and musical.

I believe in transparency. After every appointment I'll give you a plain-language summary of your piano's condition — what I found, what I did, and what to watch for over the coming months. No jargon, no upselling, just an honest assessment.

Philosophy

What I Believe About Piano Care

🎵
Tuning is seasonal

Colorado's dry climate and dramatic temperature swings affect piano pitch more than most states. Twice-yearly tuning isn't a sales pitch — it's the physics of wood and wire responding to their environment.

🔧
Regulation is underrated

Most pianists assume their instrument is as responsive as it should be. Proper regulation — adjusting the mechanical action — can transform a sluggish, uneven keyboard into one that responds to the lightest touch.

👂
Voicing shapes character

Tone is not fixed. Hammer voicing can move a piano from harsh and bright to warm and mellow, or anywhere in between. Every room and every player deserves a piano voiced for them.

📋
Honest assessments only

I will tell you when a repair isn't worth the cost, when a piano is beyond economical restoration, and when you should simply keep playing and not worry. My job is to give you information, not create work.
